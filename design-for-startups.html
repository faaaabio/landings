🔄 Methodology for startups

1. Discovery and definition

The first step is to clearly understand the problem your startup solves, who your users are, and what the business goals are.

Deliverables:

  • Discovery workshop with stakeholders
  • Map of opportunities and challenges
  • Definition of personas and priority journeys
  • Success metrics and KPIs for the product

2. Ideation and prototyping

Based on the collected information, we start creating visual and interactive solutions that can be quickly tested with real users.

Deliverables:

  • Wireframes and navigation flows
  • High-fidelity interactive prototypes
  • Usability tests with real users
  • Iterative adjustments based on feedback

3. Implementation and evolution

Close collaboration with your development team to ensure the design vision is implemented correctly and evolves based on user feedback.

Deliverables:

  • Detailed specifications for development
  • Design system to ensure consistency
  • Implementation support and visual QA
  • Recommendations for next iterations

❓ FAQs about design for startups

How much does design cost for my startup?

The investment varies depending on your startup's stage and project scope. I work with flexible models that fit different budgets, from one-off projects to ongoing collaborations. Schedule a free chat to discuss the best format for your reality.

How long does it take to create an MVP?

A well-designed MVP can be created in 4-8 weeks, depending on complexity. The focus is on quickly delivering something testable to validate hypotheses and attract investors, without compromising user experience quality and technical feasibility.

How do you work with remote teams?

I use collaborative tools like Figma, Miro, and Slack to maintain fluid communication and organized work. I establish weekly alignment and demo rituals, in addition to documenting all design decisions to keep the team synchronized.
