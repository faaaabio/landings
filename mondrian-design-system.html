Industry Telecommunications

Re-design of Digital Products with Design System

Client: Claro

Digital Transformation in Telecommunications Products Ecosystem

In 2018, I was the first member of the Design Ops team, appointed by Meiuca.co, to build the Mondrian Design System, which would be applied to Claro's digital product ecosystem during the merger with NET, a fixed telephony, broadband, and TV operator.

Project Website

Initial Challenges and Learnings

Initially, the challenge was understanding the company's organization (managers, leaders, developers, designers, and suppliers), the relationship between teams, and product objectives through qualitative research, revealing the needs and expectations for the design system.

The work focused on mapping interfaces (all digital products) and creating brand libraries, styles, component division, and processes to be tested in dynamics with business, design, and technology people, according to common objectives and priorities between teams.

The support and training provided to teams were fundamental from the beginning of the first brand style implementations to the creation of the community for library improvement, as well as bringing updates and clarifying the doubts of people involved in projects using the Mondrian Design System.

Standardizing Interfaces and Improving Productivity

After launching the libraries, training teams, and providing support, we achieved the following results:

  • 30% more productive time when using libraries;
  • Processes became more agile between designers and developers;
  • Acceptance in more than 90% of the group's teams and digital products;
  • More than 70% of visual elements were standardized and easily maintained;
  • Awarded by Cielo in the best 360° product category.
